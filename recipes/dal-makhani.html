Dal Makhani

Description

Buttery and creamy, Dal Makhani is one of the most popular dals in North India. Try our quick and easy dal makhani recipe and enjoy your creamy delight with rice or naan. Made with easily available ingredients, Dal Makhani is both flavourful and healthy. There is no occasion or festival when dal makhani is not prepared or relished. It is one of the most popular main dishes that you will see in any north Indian restaurant. If you are also a fan of Dal Makhani, then here is an easy way to make it. It’s a creamy and rich dal recipe that’s prepared using kidney beans and black whole urad dal. To increase its flavour, some people even use homemade cream along with butter for the richness. Ideally enjoyed with butter roti and naan, you can also savour this delicacy with steamed rice as well.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups soaked overnight red kidney beans
  • 1 teaspoon red chilli powder
  • 4 tablespoons butter
  • 1 large chopped onion
  • 1/2 cup tomato puree
  • 1/2 cup fresh cream
  • 1/2 teaspoon ginger paste
  • Water (as required)
  • Salt (as required)
  • 2 inches chopped ginger
  • 1 tablespoon refined oil
  • 2 medium sliced & slit green chillies
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala powder
  • 1/2 cup soaked overnight urad dal
  • 1/2 teaspoon garlic paste

Steps

  1. Soak urad, rajma overnight & pressure cook the next day

    2. To prepare this popular dish, soak sabut urad and rajma overnight in three cups of water. Drain it and pressure cook in 6 cups of water with salt as per taste. This makes the rajma and dal soft.

  2. Fry the onions, green chillies & tomato puree

    3. Take a kadhai on medium flame, then heat the oil and add the cumin seeds. Once the cumin seeds crackle, you can add half the ginger-garlic paste and stir for some time. Then add some onions, chopped green chillies and tomato puree. Fry until the mix turns golden. If you are someone who loves the authentic style of Dal Makhani, then we suggest you to use ghee instead of oil.

  3. Add the rajma & dal

    4. Once the bhuna masala is ready you can add the pressure cooked Rajma and dal, and bring to a boil. If you want your Dal Makhani to be smooth, you can grind the masala first and then add the dal and Rajma.

  4. Add the garam masala & garnish with fresh cream & coriander leaves

    5. Then add garam masala and salt as per your taste. Stir well and bring to boil, if you think that the dal is too thick, you can add some water. Then add some fresh cream to this recipe and stir well. This will make your Dal Makhani creamy and delicious. Garnish this recipe with fresh cream and freshly chopped coriander leaves.
