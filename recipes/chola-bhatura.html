Chola Bhatura

Description

A piece of hot & puffed-up bhatura with a tangy & spicy chickpea curry with salad and pickle on the side, what else can you ask for? Well, yes we are talking about the Chole Bhature recipe, which is loved by North Indians. This delicacy has Punjabi origins and thus you can imagine the taste of this dish. Here's how you can make it at home with detailed instructions explained with step-by-step images.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups soaked overnight chickpeas
  • 2 chopped onions
  • 1/4 cup tomato puree
  • 1 teaspoon coriander powder
  • 5 cloves garlic
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric
  • 1 1/2 teaspoons chana masala powder
  • 1 teaspoon cumin
  • 1 tablespoon ginger
  • 3 tablespoons onion paste
  • Salt (as required)
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala powder
  • 1 teaspoon red chilli powder

Steps

  1. Pressure cook the chickpeas

    2. To prepare the chole, soak chickpeas in water overnight or for 6-8 hours. Then, put a pressure cooker on high flame and add the soaked chickpeas in it along with salt to taste and 1 tea bag or you can also put 2-3 tea leaves and wrap it in a cloth. Cook the chickpeas for 15-20 minutes. (Note: The tea leaves or the tea bag will change the colour of gravy from dark brown to black.)

  2. Saute the onion

    3. Meanwhile, put a pan on medium flame and heat a little oil in it. Add the cumin seeds and sliced onion to the pan. Saute the onion till golden brown, then add minced garlic, ginger and onion paste. Saute the ingredients for a few seconds until the raw smell of garlic goes away and then add tomato puree.

  3. Add spices and cook the chole

    4. Once the oil is separated, add turmeric, red chilli powder, coriander powder and chana masala. Cook the tomato-onion mixture for another two minutes, and then add cooked chole with little water. Stir well to mix the masala with the chole or the chickpeas. Check salt and allow it to cook for a few minutes. The chola is done! You can garnish with chopped onion and coriander leaves.

  4. Prepare the dough

    5. Now for making the bhaturas, combine together all-purpose flour or maida and wheat flour or atta in a dough kneading plate. Then add salt and oil to it. Mix well. Add yoghurt to the flour mixture and knead the dough well. You can add a little water and knead the dough. When it is done, cover the dough with a kitchen towel and allow it to rise.

  5. Cook the bhatura and serve

    6. Now, put a kadhai on high flame and meanwhile, take equal quantities of the dough and roll big size puris (bhatura). When the oil is hot enough, carefully add these bhaturas to the oil and deep fry. Repeat with the rest of the dough. Then take a plate and serve Chola bhatura hot!
